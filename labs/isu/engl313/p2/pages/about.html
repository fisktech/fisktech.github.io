Meet Hans Peter Luhn

Hans Peter Luhn was a German computer scientist who worked at IBM for many years. Luhn invented many systems still in use today, and was awarded over 80 patents.

History of Luhn's Algorithm

Luhn's Algorithm was created back in 1954.

The algorithm is used to validate many different identification numbers, such as credit cards, IMEI numbers, National Provider Identification Numbers, Canadian and Greek Social Security Numbers, and many more.

Luhn's algorithm is intended to check for human input errors, and is not meant to protect against malicious attacks.

Today, Luhn's Algorithm is in the public domain and is used by many systems worldwide. If you have ever purchased anything online, you have very likely encountered Luhn's Algorithm.
